Stall holders terms and conditions (Please read)

All stall holders will be responsible for holding the correct insurance, you will be asked to provide a copy, and will be checked on the day. Personal Liability insurance with a minimum value of £5 million is needed.

All stall holders are responsible for their own waste clearing during and after the event. Providing bins for your customers, keeping your area tidy and ensuring all rubbish is taken off site and disposed of correctly, including any cooking oil and materials.

All stalls are pre-allocated, at the size indicated in your booking. Any requests can you made when you are contacted, however cannot be guaranteed. Any issues surrounding the pre-allocated space or sizing, may result in removal from the site by security.

A refundable £25 deposit is required to book a stall. This will be fully refundable subject to all terms and conditions being adhered to, 5 – 10 business days after the event.

All stall holders will be required to arrive with any vehicles between 8am – 10am on the day of the event, outside of those times, you will not be able to gain access to the area with a vehicle due to safety of participants. Stall holders will not be able to bring their vehicles back on to the site until 8pm at the earliest, when security has deemed it safe to do so.

No vehicles are to be kept on the site itself, unless pre-authorised, due to safety of participants. Pre-authorisation reasons may include you vehicle being part of your stall, for example a donut van, in which case you will be asked to provide a copy of all relevant insurance, and will be checked on the day.

When using a vehicle to set up on the day, we ask that you have your confirmation ready for the team to be able to direct you to your pre-allocated slot as quickly as possible. On entering the site, you must travel within a 5mph speed limit and be aware of pedestrians, bike riders and animals at all times.

The Eid Festival has a zero-tolerance policy to the sale of alcohol, illegal substances or selling and displaying counterfeit goods. If you are found to be in possession of any of these, you will be removed from the site immediately.

You are responsible for the health and safety and wellbeing of your employees and public in the vicinity of your stall. Please take into consideration adverse weather conditions when planning for the day.

All goods and personal belongings is the responsibility of the vendor. The Eid Festival accepts no responsibility for theft, damage or lost items.
